A & E department
A 30-year-old man arrives at hospital 40 minutes after being involved in a motor vehicle accident. He has already received 1000 mls of plasma substitute during transport. He is unconsciousness and responds only to pain. There are no focal neurological signs. There is stridor, intercostal retraction and no evidence of pneumothorax pulse rate is 140/min. blood pressure is 70 mmHg. Systolic and the abdomen is rapidly distending. There is a right femoral shaft fracture and there is ischaemia of the lower right leg. Before proceeding to operating theatre for laparotomy this patient was stabilised with IV fluids.
